Wheel of Fortune fans claim player was robbed on bonus puzzle she clearly solved as view

 Posted on September 23, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  608 words  |  Aldo Pusey

WHEEL of Fortune fans have erupted in fury over a contentious judgment call on Tuesday's episode.

Many viewers took to social media, arguing that contestant Megan was unjustly denied a $40,000 prize for correctly solving the bonus puzzle.

The California-based teacher, married to her husband Michael for a decade and raising two boys, triumphed with a total of $14,007.

As she headed into the bonus round, Megan, exclaiming, "I'm so excited!" chose "Living Thing" as her category.

In addition to RSTLNE, Megan chose "CDPA" to help her with the two-word puzzle, which read: “P_N_’ ‘_RC_ _ D’

When the 10-second timer commenced, her immediate guess sounded like either "Pink Orchid" or a very quiet "Something Orchid."

Undeterred, she continued with "Pine Orchid?" only to face disappointment when the full puzzle was revealed as "Pink Orchid."

Host Pat Sajak, 77, expressed regret, stating, "You might have been overthinking a type of orchid; I’m sorry that didn't work out for you."

The revelation of a missed $40,000 from the glitzy prize card prompted Megan to exclaim, "Aw!"

However, hoards of fans challenged the game show's decision, insisting, to their ears, that Megan had indeed said "Pink Orchid" in her initial guess.

This isn't the only time that fans have recently called Wheel of Fortune's bonus round into question.

Earlier this month, fans accused the iconic game show of manipulating the prizes the prize round to ensure a BMW was won by the end of the week.

All five winners landed on the same BMW prize wedge in the bonus round in a single week, which, by night five, appeared more than just coincidental.

One fan took to X, writing: "OK, is Wheel of Fortune scamming us? How are they spinning the BMW 5 nights in a row?"

Another fan quipped, "I firmly believe that every prize on the wheel in the bonus round is a BMW."
