Biography

Masoja Josiah Msiza (born 5th October 1964), addressed officially as Masoja Msiza, is a talented South African actor, poet, and musician.

Born in the township of KwaThema, Gauteng, South Africa, he began to show interest in arts from an early age.

Masoja Msiza is best known for portraying “Nkunzi Mhlongo” in the award-winning telenovela Uzalo.

Early Life & Education

Masoja Msiza was born on October 5, 1964, in the KwaThema, Gauteng, South Africa. 

Aged 59, he is a Zulu man from a family of eleven children. From a young age, he loved the arts and performance.

This led him to study drama at the University of KwaZulu-Natal.

Personal Life

According to multiple reports, Masoja Msiza was married to Innocentia Msiza. Sadly, their marriage hit the rock for a reason he has never known to the public. Their union is blessed with three children, which comprises a son and two daughters.

In an Instagram post, Masoja Msiza revealed how he missed his family; in his words, he said,

“I miss my kids‚ I miss all of those close to me. I see them when I come to Johannesburg‚, like in the December holidays. I am used to that because my relatives raised my siblings and me. I would see them at Christmas time.  

I wanted the cycle to end with me. I have suffered, and I thought it would end with me, but I did not think it would happen to my children‚ that I would go through a series of divorces. It was beyond my control”, he narrated.

Career

Masoja Msiza used to work as a miner after finishing school, but he was sacked along with other miners involved in a strike. Following his dismissal, he chose to follow his ambition of becoming an actor, and his first role was in a play directed by Gibson Kente called “Mfowethu.”

Masoja Msiza started as a poet and then worked as a theater and screen actor, musician, and narrator. He has featured in several notable films, including Kalushi: Solomon Mahlangu’s Story and A Million Colors.

Nevertheless, his best-known character is that of ruthless crime boss Nkunzebomvu “Nkunzi” Mhlongo on South Africa’s most popular television show, Uzalo.

 Scandal!, Shreads and Dreams, Rhythm City, Intersexions, Sokhulu & Partners, and Making Cents with the Sitholes are some of his TV appearances.
