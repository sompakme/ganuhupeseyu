AOMG Artists Heading to the West for Their 2022 USA Tour 'ABOVE ORDINARY'

In partnership with KOHAI, South Korea record label AOMG is set to return to the United States for their "ABOVE ORDINARY" tour, which will feature five of its talented singers: Woo, DeVita, Coogie, DJ Wegun, and sogumm.

This will be the first time in three years that the Korean entertainment agency will hold a concert tour in the US since their "ABOVE ORDINARY USA & Canada Tour 2019."

The five AOMG artists will bring great performances in a total of 10 cities: Austin, Dallas, Houston, Atlanta, Minneapolis, New York City, Chicago, Seattle, Los Angeles, and San Francisco.

About South Korean Label AOMG

The term "AOMG" is an acronym of Above Ordinary Music Group. This Korean hip-hop and R&B record label was founded by singer, songwriter, and rapper Jay Park.

The agency derives from the "Art of Movement," a Seattle-based B-Boying group that Jay joined in 2003 and consists of Cha Cha Malone, Dial Tone, and Junior.

(Photo : News 1)
Former AOMG CEO, Jay Park

Established in late September 2013, AOMG signed composer Jun Goon, Seattle affiliate Cha Cha Malone, and singer and producer Gray.

Two years later, the hip-hop company signed with several artists, such as Loco, DJ Wegun, Hoody, Ugly Duck, Elo, and DJ Pumpkin.

In March 2014, AOMG welcomed Simon Dominic as the label's co-CEO, but he later stepped down from the position in 2018.

In August 2019, singer-songwriter sogumm joined AOMG after winning first place in the agency's first hip-hop audition show "Signhere."
